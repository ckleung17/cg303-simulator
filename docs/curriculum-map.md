# Unit 303 curriculum map

This is a design mapping, not an official assessment specification. Maintainers
must verify it against the current qualification handbook used by the learner's
centre before release.

## LO1: Health and safety requirements

Simulator evidence:

- Risk controls, permits/method information and communication before work.
- PPE, barriers and warning notices where appropriate.
- Safe isolation with a suitable voltage indicator and proving unit.
- Correct instruments/leads and handling of unsafe situations.

Assessment evidence: ordered actions, omitted controls, unsafe attempts and the
learner's explanation of precautions.

## LO2: Reporting and communication

Simulator evidence:

- Customer complaint and clarification dialogue.
- Relevant people notified about circuit/equipment loss and restoration.
- Documentation consulted and final findings recorded accurately.

Assessment evidence: information selected, notifications, diagnostic notes and
completion report.

## LO3: Nature and characteristics of faults

Simulator evidence:

- Symptoms and readings caused by loss of supply, low voltage/voltage drop,
  equipment failure, protective-device operation, arcing/high resistance,
  overload, insulation failure, open/short/earth faults and signal/control faults.
- Fault locations across wiring, terminations, accessories, switchgear, control
  equipment and metering/instrumentation where implemented.

Assessment evidence: initial hypotheses, predicted effects and final diagnosis.

## LO4: Fault-diagnosis procedure

Simulator evidence:

- Identify symptoms, gather documents/history and analyse information.
- Select a fit-for-purpose instrument and confirm its operation.
- Choose a logical sequence of continuity, insulation resistance, polarity,
  loop/PFC, RCD, current/voltage, phase-sequence and functional tests as relevant.
- Interpret results against scenario-specific expectations.

Assessment evidence: sequence efficiency, appropriateness, readings and written
interpretation. The tool catalogue must not imply every test suits every case.

## LO5: Correcting faults

Simulator evidence:

- Choose repair, replacement, escalation or isolation with consideration of
  cost, availability, downtime, responsibility, access and continuity of supply.
- Select suitable post-correction verification.
- Leave the simulated area safe and account for waste/disposal.

Assessment evidence: recommendation, justification, verification plan and close
out actions.

## LO6: Perform fault diagnosis

Simulator evidence:

- Complete an end-to-end, safe diagnostic exercise in a simulated environment.
- Use symptoms, information and tests to reach an evidence-based diagnosis.
- Recommend appropriate corrective action.

Assessment evidence: holistic scenario score with safety gates. This outcome
must not be satisfied by multiple-choice questions alone.

## Coverage data

Each curated scenario and generated scenario template should declare:

- `learningOutcomes`
- `circuitTypes`
- `faultFamilies`
- `instruments`
- `testMethods`
- `safetyControls`
- `communicationEvents`
- `correctionFactors`
- `reviewStatus` and review metadata

The learner dashboard should derive coverage from completed evidence, not merely
from scenarios opened.
